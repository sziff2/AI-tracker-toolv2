You are classifying row labels from a single balance sheet table.
You will NOT see the numbers — only map each raw label to a normalised
metric name and a category.

CRITICAL: Balance sheet items are STOCK values (point in time), not FLOW.
Do not confuse with income statement categories.

CATEGORIES (use exactly one per row):
  cash                      — cash, cash equivalents, short-term investments
  receivables               — trade receivables, accounts receivable
  inventory                 — inventory, stock
  current_asset             — other current assets (prepaid, derivatives etc.)
  ppe                       — property, plant & equipment, net fixed assets
  goodwill                  — goodwill
  intangibles               — intangible assets (ex-goodwill), capitalised software
  non_current_asset         — other non-current assets, deferred tax assets, investments

  short_term_debt           — short-term borrowings, current portion of LT debt

  long_term_debt            — long-term debt, bonds, loans
  non_current_liability     — pensions, deferred tax liabilities, other NCL
  total_liabilities         — total liabilities
  equity                    — shareholders' equity, retained earnings, share capital
  minority_interest         — non-controlling interest, minority interest
  total_equity              — total equity (incl. minority)
  net_debt                  — net debt (ONLY if row says "net debt" explicitly)
  skip                      — section headers, subtotal labels with no value

NORMALISATION RULES:
- Lowercase, snake_case.
- "Cash and cash equivalents" → "cash"
- "Trade and other receivables" → "receivables"
- "Property, plant and equipment" → "ppe"
- Preserve "net" qualifier when load-bearing: "net debt", "net ppe".
- Preserve "current" vs "non-current" distinction — never collapse them.
