You are classifying row labels from a single income statement table.
You will NOT see the numbers — your only job is to map each raw label
to a normalised metric name and a category.

CATEGORIES (use exactly one per row):
  revenue            — top line, net sales, turnover, total revenue
  cost_of_sales      — COGS, cost of revenue, cost of goods sold
  gross_profit       — gross profit / gross margin (absolute, not %)
  operating_expense  — SG&A, R&D, marketing, admin, distribution, D&A inside opex
  operating_profit   — EBIT, operating income, trading profit
  ebitda             — EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA, underlying EBITDA
  ebit               — EBIT (only if reported separately from operating profit)
  interest           — net finance cost, interest expense, interest income
  tax                — income tax expense, current tax, deferred tax
  net_income         — net profit, profit for the period, earnings, net earnings
  eps                — basic EPS, diluted EPS, adjusted EPS
  shares_outstanding — basic shares, diluted shares, weighted avg shares
  dps                — dividends per share, dividend declared
  other_income       — anything that doesn't fit above
  skip               — subtotal labels with no value, section headers, blank rows

NORMALISATION RULES:
- Lowercase, snake_case the normalised name.
- Strip "total", "consolidated" prefixes UNLESS load-bearing
  (e.g. "net debt" ≠ "debt" — keep "net").
- Map synonyms to a canonical name:
    "Net sales", "Turnover", "Total revenue"   → "revenue"
    "Profit after tax", "Profit for the year"  → "net_income"
    "Operating income", "Trading profit"       → "operating_profit"
- Preserve "adjusted" / "underlying" / "reported" qualifiers in the
  normalised name (e.g. "adjusted_ebitda", "reported_operating_profit").
  These distinctions matter — do not collapse them.

EDGE CASES:
- Section headers with no value (e.g. "Operating expenses:") → category="skip".
- Per-share items always go to "eps" / "shares_outstanding" / "dps",
  never "net_income", even though they're derived from it.
- Ambiguous "Other" rows: use surrounding context; if unclear → "other_income".

OUTPUT:
Return ONLY a JSON array. One object per input label, in the same order.
Each object MUST have these three keys:
  {{"original": "...", "normalised": "...", "category": "..."}}

EXAMPLE
Input:
- Net sales
- Cost of goods sold
- Gross profit
- Selling, general and administrative
- Operating income
- Adjusted EBITDA
- Net income attributable to shareholders
- Diluted earnings per share

  {{"original": "Net sales",                                "normalised": "revenue",           "category": "revenue"}},

  {{"original": "Gross profit",                             "normalised": "gross_profit",      "category": "gross_profit"}},
  {{"original": "Selling, general and administrative",      "normalised": "sga",               "category": "operating_expense"}},
  {{"original": "Operating income",                         "normalised": "operating_profit",  "category": "operating_profit"}},
  {{"original": "Adjusted EBITDA",                          "normalised": "adjusted_ebitda",   "category": "ebitda"}},
  {{"original": "Net income attributable to shareholders",  "normalised": "net_income",        "category": "net_income"}},
